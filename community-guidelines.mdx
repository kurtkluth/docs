AI Builders Circle is built on the idea that everyone learns faster when the community shares freely. These guidelines keep the space helpful, respectful, and on-topic so you get maximum value from every interaction. They're short, they're common sense, and they exist to protect the quality of the community for every member — including you.

## The Rules

1. **Be helpful and respectful** — Treat every member the way you'd want to be treated. Constructive feedback is welcome; dismissiveness and negativity are not.
2. **No spam or unsolicited self-promotion** — You're encouraged to share your wins, projects, and tools you love. Don't use the community as an ad channel or blast promotional messages to other members.
3. **Stay on topic** — Keep conversations focused on AI, automation, and building for business. This isn't a general chat room — the more focused the discussion, the more useful it is for everyone.
4. **Give more than you take** — Answer questions when you can, share templates you've built, and celebrate other members' wins. The best communities are ones where people contribute before they ask.

## Community Channels

The community is organized into four core channels. Here's what each one is for:

<Accordion title="General Discussion 💭">
  The open forum for ideas, questions, and conversations about AI and automation. If something doesn't fit neatly into another channel, post it here. This is also a great place to share articles, tools you've discovered, or thoughts on where AI is heading.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Ask for Help 🧠">
  Stuck on a workflow, an API connection, a prompt that won't behave, or a tool you can't figure out? Post here and the community will help you troubleshoot. Be specific — share what you tried, what happened, and what you expected. The more context you give, the faster you'll get a useful answer.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Wins + Results 🏆">
  Share what you built, saved, or earned this week. Did you automate a task that was eating three hours of your day? Ship an AI-powered tool? Land a client using a workflow you built here? Post it. Momentum is contagious, and your win might be exactly the spark someone else needs.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Free Resources 🎁">
  Templates, prompts, cheat sheets, and links shared by Josh and community members. This channel is updated regularly — check back often or keep an eye on notifications. If you have something worth sharing, drop it here for everyone to use.
</Accordion>

## Getting the Most Out of the Community

A little intentionality goes a long way. Here are a few habits that separate people who get a lot out of communities like this from people who don't:

- **Show up consistently.** Even spending 10 minutes a few times a week — answering one question, sharing one resource — compounds quickly over months.
- **Ask specific questions.** "How do I use n8n?" is hard to answer. "How do I pass a variable from an HTTP node into a Code node in n8n?" is easy to answer.
- **Share your work in progress.** You don't need a finished product to post in Wins + Results. A workflow that half-works is worth sharing — you'll get feedback that helps you finish it.
- **Use the Classroom before you ask.** Josh has put together structured resources in the Classroom tab. Check there first — your question may already be answered with a full walkthrough.

<Tip>
  If you're ever unsure whether something belongs in the community, default to sharing it. The worst that can happen is someone redirects you to a better channel — and you'll still have started a conversation.
</Tip>
